Frequently Asked Questions

→ What is WineCellarManager?

WineCellarManager is a free online wine cellar management software. It allows you to track your wine collection, record tasting notes, manage your inventory, and much more.

→ How much does it cost?

WineCellarManager is completely free to use. Just register and start managing your wine collection today.

→ What information can I track?

For each wine you can track: name, vintage, producer, type, appellation, up to 4 varietals, bottle size, alcohol percentage, and a description. You can also record acquisition information including date, quantity, price, where you bought it, storage location, and whether it's on order.

→ Can I record tasting notes?

Yes! For each wine you can add detailed tasting notes covering appearance, nose, palate, finish, and overall impression, along with a score from 1 to 100.

→ What is the "Ready to Drink" feature?

When you add wines to your cellar, you can set a drinking window (drink from year / drink to year). The "Ready to Drink" view shows you all wines currently in their optimal drinking window, so you never miss a wine at its peak.

→ Can I see statistics about my cellar?

Yes, the Cellar Statistics page gives you a one-page overview of your cellar including total bottles, total value, average price, breakdowns by type, appellation, and varietal, plus your top rated wines.

→ Can I export my cellar data?

Yes, you can export your entire wine collection as a CSV file at any time using the Cellar Export feature.

→ What is Quick Entry?

Quick Entry allows you to add multiple wines at once in a rapid-fire form, perfect for when you're cataloging a large collection for the first time.

→ What are Best Wine Values?

The Best Wine Values page ranks your cellar wines by their value score (rating divided by price). This helps you find the best-performing wines in your collection relative to what you paid.

→ Can I share my cellar with friends?

Each user has their own private cellar. You can invite friends to register and build their own collections.
